About Grow Life Homesteading

A community-driven organization built on the belief that growing your own food is one of the most powerful things a person can do.

Our Mission
❝

At Grow Life, we want to give people back the power of what it means to grow their own organic food and live a more sustainable and healthier lifestyle regardless of time, physical capability, or educational means to maximize their harvests each year. We want to revolutionize the food market and reconnect people to their natural state of living. Grow Life is about building a community, living closer to the Earth, and reacquainting ourselves with the people around us because food is something that will always bring us all together to the table.

— Grow Life Homesteading

What Goes In the Ground

Soil Amendments We Use

Healthy soil is the foundation of everything. We feed the soil, and the soil feeds your plants. Every amendment we use is chosen to build long-term fertility — not just a quick fix.

🌱

Compost & Vermicompost

The backbone of our soil program. Finished compost and worm castings deliver a broad spectrum of nutrients, beneficial microbes, and organic matter that dramatically improves soil structure and water retention.

🔥

Biochar

A highly porous carbon structure that acts as a long-term habitat for beneficial soil microbes. Biochar improves drainage in heavy soils, boosts water retention in sandy soils, and sequesters carbon for decades.

⛰️

Rock Dust & Minerals

Glacial rock dust and mineral blends remineralize soils that have been depleted over time. Trace minerals are the difference between food that merely grows and food that truly nourishes.

🍄

Mycorrhizal Inoculants

We inoculate root zones with mycorrhizal fungi to build the underground network that plants depend on. This dramatically extends root reach, improves drought resistance, and increases nutrient uptake — naturally.

🌊

Kelp & Fish Meal

Ocean-derived inputs rich in naturally chelated minerals, growth hormones, and amino acids. Kelp meal stimulates microbial activity and plant immunity. Fish meal delivers slow-release nitrogen that feeds without burning.

🌿

Bone & Blood Meal

Bone meal provides slow-release phosphorus for strong root development and flowering. Blood meal is a fast-acting nitrogen source used strategically to kickstart growth during key periods without synthetic intervention.

🌾

Cover Crop Biomass

We strategically use cover crops — clover, rye, buckwheat, vetch — to fix nitrogen, suppress weeds, and chop-and-drop as green manure. This is living soil building in real time.

🔄

Composting Methods

We don't just pile and hope. Our composting approach is intentional and layered — hot composting to kill pathogens and weed seeds, vermicomposting for the richest castings, and cold composting for long-term slow decomposition. We also take sourcing seriously — all materials we bring in are vetted to ensure they are free of PFAS and other harmful forever chemicals.

  • Hot composting — 130–160°F kill zones
  • Vermicomposting with red wigglers
  • Chop-and-drop composting in place
  • Compost tea brewing for soil drenches
  • All sourced materials vetted PFAS-free
🌿

Cover Cropping & Soil Health

Bare soil is a problem. We keep the ground covered year-round using strategic cover crop mixes tailored to your region and season. This suppresses weeds, prevents erosion, feeds the soil web, and builds organic matter without any inputs from off-site.

  • Nitrogen-fixing legume blends
  • Crimson clover for nitrogen fixation & pollinators
  • Winter rye & hairy vetch for cold seasons
  • Buckwheat for fast summer coverage
  • Daikon radish for compaction breaking
  • Pollinator-friendly mixes between beds
🐛

Pest & Disease Management (IPM)

We use Integrated Pest Management — meaning we work with nature before reaching for any spray, even an organic one. Healthy soil grows resilient plants. Beneficial insects, companion planting, and physical barriers handle the rest. Intervention is targeted and minimal.

  • Beneficial insect habitat plantings
  • Companion planting for pest confusion
  • Neem oil & insecticidal soap as last resort
  • Row covers & physical exclusion
  • Crop rotation to break disease cycles
  • Fermented plant juice foliar sprays
💧

Water Management

We design every property to catch, slow, and sink water before it leaves the land. Swales on contour, rain gardens, and pond systems transform rain from a runoff problem into your most valuable resource. Drip irrigation and deep mulching minimize what you use.

  • Swales & earthworks on contour
  • Rainwater harvesting & storage systems
  • Pond & natural swimming hole construction
  • Drip irrigation & sub-surface delivery
  • Deep mulching for water retention
  • Rain garden & bioswale design
📅

Seasonal Maintenance

A productive homestead requires a rhythm. We maintain seasonal schedules tailored to your specific property, climate, and crops — so nothing falls through the cracks and your land is always one step ahead.

  • Spring: soil prep, early planting, pruning
  • Summer: irrigation management, succession planting
  • Fall: harvest, cover cropping, soil amendment
  • Winter: infrastructure repair, season extension
  • Year-round: compost turning, pest scouting

Animal Integration

Animals are a core part of a functioning homestead ecosystem. We integrate chickens strategically to cycle nutrients, manage pests, and build soil fertility — using proven systems like chicken tractors and chicken tunnels to put their natural behaviors to work on your land.

  • Chicken tractors for garden prep & pest control
  • Chicken tunnels to direct flock movement between beds
  • Rotational grazing & paddock design
  • Manure composting & nutrient cycling
  • Coop placement for maximum land benefit
🌳

Permaculture Design Principles

Permaculture isn't a technique — it's a lens. We design every property as an integrated system where each element serves multiple functions and every output becomes an input somewhere else. The result is a landscape that becomes more productive, more resilient, and less dependent on outside inputs every single year.

Observe First

We study your land before we touch it — sun, wind, water flow, and soil.

Stack Functions

Every plant, structure, and animal serves at least three purposes in the system.

Catch Energy

Water, sunlight, wind, and biomass — we design to capture and use each one.

Use Edges

The most productive zones are edges. We maximize them in every design.
